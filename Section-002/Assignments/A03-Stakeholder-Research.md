# Stakeholder Research

## Overview

Conduct distributed empathy research as a team during the two-week research period (March 12-26). Each team member observes or interviews stakeholders relevant to your scenario in their own context, then the team synthesizes findings into a single document. Your combined reach across different workplaces, industries, and communities produces richer data than any single-context study.

## Learning Objectives

- Conduct empathy research (observation and/or interviews) with real stakeholders
- Coordinate distributed research across a team with diverse access points
- Synthesize findings from multiple contexts into coherent insights
- Identify patterns that emerge across different environments

## What to Do

1. **Each team member conducts 1-2 research activities** in contexts they can access:
   - Observation (45-60 min): Observe stakeholders using the AEIOU framework (Activities, Environments, Interactions, Objects, Users). Document in notebook, transcribe key findings for team.
   - Interview (30-45 min): Interview 1-2 people from relevant stakeholder groups about their experience, constraints, workarounds, and frustrations. Summarize key quotes and insights.
2. **Coordinate as a team.** Divide stakeholder types based on access. Check in asynchronously during the two weeks. Share findings in a team document before Session 4.
3. **Compile a team synthesis document** with four sections:
   - Research Overview: Which stakeholders, what contexts, what methods
   - Individual Contributions: Context, 3-5 key observations or quotes, and surprises per member
   - Cross-Context Patterns: What patterns emerged across members' research? Where did different contexts reveal different facets?
   - Team Stakeholder Map: A new map grounded in your findings (not a revision of earlier maps). For each stakeholder: what they optimize for, what constrains them.

Individual research notes belong in your notebook and will be assessed as part of your notebook grade.

## Submit

Team synthesis document (PDF) to Canvas before Session 4 (Thursday, March 26).

## Specifications

**1. Research Breadth and Depth**
Met: Each team member contributed at least one substantial research activity. Research covers multiple stakeholder types with 45+ minutes of engagement per member and specific, detailed findings.

**2. Distributed Context Advantage**
Met: Team leveraged members' diverse contexts to access different stakeholders or environments. Document shows how different access points contributed unique perspectives.

**3. Synthesis Quality**
Met: Cross-context patterns section identifies meaningful insights from comparing findings across members. Team stakeholder map reflects research findings, not initial assumptions.

**4. Evidence of Genuine Research**
Met: Includes specific quotes, observations, or details that could only come from actual research. Surprises or assumption-challenges are noted.

**5. Documentation Completeness**
Met: All four sections present and complete with sufficient depth and detail.
