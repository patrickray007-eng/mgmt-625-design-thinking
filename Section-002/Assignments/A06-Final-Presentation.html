Final Presentation: Experiment Results

What to Do

8-9 minute presentation. Tell us what you tested and what you learned.

  1. Problem and hypothesis (2 min): What's the problem? What did you hypothesize? What assumption did you test?
  2. The experiment (2 min): What did you actually do? Who did you test with? How did you measure?
  3. Results (2-3 min): What happened? What surprised you? What broke?
  4. What's next (2 min): What would you do differently? What's the next experiment?

Honest reporting matters more than successful experiments.
