# Final Presentation

## Overview

Present your team's problem-to-solution arc in an 8-10 minute presentation, demonstrating how empathy work shaped your understanding and led to your solution hypotheses. This is a demonstration of design thinking in action, not a polished pitch. The goal is traceability: research led to insights, insights shaped the problem, the problem guided solutions. A 4-5 minute Q&A/stress test follows.

## Learning Objectives

- Communicate a complex problem-to-solution narrative clearly and concisely
- Demonstrate traceability from empathy work to solution concepts
- Present evidence-based reasoning to an audience
- Receive and respond to feedback constructively

## What to Do

1. **Present the problem (2-3 min).** Who experiences it, why it matters, what makes it hard to solve (tensions, feedback loops).
2. **Show your research journey (3-4 min).** How your team leveraged distributed research, what you learned that you didn't expect, 2-3 concrete examples (quotes, observations, moments that mattered).
3. **Present your solution hypotheses (2-3 min).** 1-2 solution concepts, how each connects to specific research insights, key assumptions, what you'd test first with more time.
4. **Participate in Q&A and stress testing (4-5 min).** Classmates will pressure-test your logic, identify missed assumptions, and surface alternative explanations. It's okay to say "we don't know, that's an assumption we'd need to test."

Use your [[A05-Problem-Definition|traceability map]] and favor visuals (stakeholder maps, causal loops, research photos) over text-heavy slides.

## Submit

Present in class Session 5 (Saturday, April 4), 8-10 minutes plus 4-5 min Q&A. Upload slides or visual aids to Canvas after session.

## Specifications

**1. Problem Clarity**
Met: Problem is clearly articulated. Audience understands who experiences it, what it is, and why it matters. Bounded scope with explanation of why the problem persists.

**2. Research Evidence**
Met: 2-3 concrete examples from team research (specific quotes, observations, insights). Demonstrates how distributed research across different contexts added value.

**3. Traceability**
Met: Clear logic chain from research to insights to problem to solution hypotheses. Audience can follow how specific insights led to specific solution directions.

**4. Assumption Awareness**
Met: Key assumptions underlying solution hypotheses are identified. Team acknowledges what they don't know and can articulate what they'd test next.

**5. Presentation Delivery**
Met: 8-10 minutes, all team members contribute, organized and easy to follow, visual aids support the narrative.

**6. Engagement with Feedback**
Met: Team engages constructively with Q&A and stress testing. Answers are grounded in research and team can distinguish evidence from assumptions.
