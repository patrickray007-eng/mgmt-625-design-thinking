Causal Loop Example

From a TETHER observation at 99 Ranch Food Court, Houston

Reinforcing (R1, R2)
Balancing (B1, B2)
(+) same direction   (−) opposite direction

The Observation

An elderly man with an unsteady gait and a tall cane enters the food court. He steadies himself on retractable barrier posts spaced one foot apart. There is no break in the perimeter barrier. He sits alone at a four-top with no food. A younger companion joins him and shares food. Meanwhile, up to 20 people crowd around the duck stall's hot case.

Who Benefits?

The food court operators. Two balancing loops mask the reinforcing cycles: the companion provides a social workaround (B1), and the barriers themselves accidentally provide physical support (B2). The design's success at attracting crowds adds a second reinforcing pathway (R2). The companion and the mobility-limited visitor absorb the cost that the system externalizes. Multiple workarounds converging on the same variable signals a systemic design gap.
