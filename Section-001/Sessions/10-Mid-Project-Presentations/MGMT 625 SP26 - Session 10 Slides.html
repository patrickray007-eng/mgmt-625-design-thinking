Professional Design Critique

This is how design teams at IDEO and Google present work-in-progress. Three Design Abilities in play today:

Communicate Deliberately
Your station tells the story without you narrating it. The docent listens and responds, but does not present.
Build & Craft Intentionally
You chose what to include, what to leave out, and how to arrange it. Those are craft decisions about resolution.
Concrete ↔ Abstract
Show both levels. Concrete: quotes, data, causal loop. Abstract: problem definition, the "because" connecting research to concept.

Show Your Thinking

7 stations  |  ~4.5 minutes per station  |  Leave written feedback at each

At each station, look for:

  1. Problem definition that is bounded, specific, and traceable to research
  2. Research highlights: stakeholder quotes, observations, causal loop map
  3. Top 1-2 solution concepts connected to specific insights

Written feedback prompts:

  • I like... (what's working, where the logic is strong)
  • I wish... (what you'd change or want more of)
  • I wonder... (questions, curiosities, what-ifs)

Docents

First half (~2 min): Silence. Visitors read. Docent observes but does not speak.

Second half (~2 min): Q&A. Visitors ask questions. Docent responds, but does not present.

Halfway: Swap docents so everyone gets time in both roles.

Final round: Open visit for docents to see stations they missed.

What Did We Learn?

  • Docents: What did visitors need you to explain that the materials didn't?
  • Where was the evidence chain from research to concept clearest?
  • What patterns did you notice across stations?
  • Where did the logic break down, or where did a concept feel ungrounded?
  • What was the most useful piece of feedback your team received?

What's Next
