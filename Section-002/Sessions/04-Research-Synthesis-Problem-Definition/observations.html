Field Observations: Voting Access in the LGBT Community

5 observers across Houston's Montrose neighborhood — 45 raw observations
A — Bar (weeknight)
B — Resource center
C — Coffee shop
D — Bar (weekend)
E — Support group
1. A Guy, mid-30s. Votes every election. "Never had a problem getting to a polling place. That's not the issue." "They don't see us."
2. B Bulletin board, ~15 flyers. Health services, legal aid, voter reg drive partially covered by a drag show flyer.
3. C Overheard: "I'm not giving them my address again. Last time I updated my registration my roommate opened county mail."
4. D 9:47 PM. 34 people. Astros on TV, sound off. Tip jar: "PROTECT TRANS KIDS" sticker, bail fund sticker.
5. A Woman, late 40s. "I did everything right in 2020. Volunteered, drove people to polls. Then the legislature just..." "I'm tired."
6. B Front desk: condoms, Trevor Project cards, "Know your rights at a traffic stop" sign. No voter reg materials.
7. E Support group, ~12 people. Facilitator asked what everyone did for themselves this week. Nothing political came up.
8. C Bookstore display: "RESIST." Trans memoir, Stonewall history, tenant organizing zine. Nothing about voting.
9. D Guy, early 20s, to bartender: "My parents don't know where I live. You think I'm putting my real address on a government form?"
10. A Couple, both men, 50s. Same polling place 15 years. "The poll workers know us now. First few years you could feel them looking."
11. B Woman helping younger person (~19) fill out forms. Younger person kept looking around. Woman moved closer, blocking the view.
12. E Person, mid-20s, stocks community fridges at three Montrose locations. "That's my politics. I can see it."
13. C Barista, trans flag pin. Registered but: "I moved from Beaumont and I don't know if my registration followed me." Moved on to next customer.
14. D Restroom stickers: "ALL GENDERS WELCOME." "NOBODY IS COMING TO SAVE US." Someone wrote under it: "good."
15. A Bartender, trans woman. "I vote but I hate it. My ID still has my old name." Can't afford the name change.
16. B Hallway mural, Houston skyline. "WE TAKE CARE OF US." Shelf below: PrEP, housing assistance, hate crime reporting.
17. E Facilitator asked about "the political climate." Responses: "Exhausted." "Numb." "I don't feel anything about it anymore and that scares me."
18. C Two guys, laptops. "Did you see the new bill?" "Which one." "The bathroom one." "Yeah."
19. D 10:38 PM. Tracked topics at a table of 5 for ~15 min. Work (3), dating apps (5), surgery (2), rent (4), politics (1, changed subject).
20. A Black man, 60s. "I vote because people died for me to vote. That's not about whether it works." Represented? "I'm a Black gay man in Texas. I've never felt represented. I just show up."
21. B Waiting area. Person on clipboard forms. Staff called a name. Person left pen on chair. Other person put it on table without looking up.
22. E Woman, 40s. "Voting is one tool. It's not even the sharpest one." Runs a legal network for ICE encounters. "I can see the results the same week."
23. C Tip jar collecting for someone's top surgery. Customer put in $5. Barista: "That's for Marcus, he's almost there."
24. D 11:01 PM. TV switched to drag performance. Nobody looked up. AC struggling. 80 degrees outside.
25. A Woman, 22, never voted. "The things that affect my life, nobody's voting on those. Housing. Safety. Whether my insurance covers my hormones. That's offices, not ballot boxes."
26. B Sign above computers: "NEED HELP WITH: Name change forms, Housing applications, SNAP benefits, Voter registration." That order.
27. E Re: council member at pride. "She shows up for the photo op then votes against our interests." "At least she shows up. When's the last time any of them came without a camera?"
28. C Stall door: DV hotline for queer relationships. Below in Sharpie: "this saved my life." Different hand: "mine too."
29. D Two women on a zoning vote for a queer youth shelter. One knew every council member's position. "I went to every meeting. All six."
30. A Guy, 40s, legal advocacy. "Everyone asks about voting. Nobody asks about the 400 hours we spent fighting a reg that would've closed three queer businesses."
31. B Staff on phone 20 min. Caught: "No, you don't need a permanent address... a shelter address works." Hung up, added a sticky note to a wall of ~30 sticky notes.
32. E Group discussing mutual aid Venmo chain. "It's just us looking out for us." Someone had a spreadsheet tracking who'd been helped.
33. C Woman, 70s. "Voted every election since 1972." Made a difference? "It made a difference for marriage. But I waited 40 years for that one."
34. D 10:22 PM. Alt-weekly by the door. Front page: Montrose gentrification. Nobody picked one up in 25 min.
35. A Overheard: "If you don't vote you can't complain." "I organize, show up to hearings, donate, protest. You fill in a bubble once a year and think that's the whole thing?"
36. B Calendar: HIV testing, trans support group, know-your-rights workshop, clothing swap, mixer. No voter events.
37. E Person, ~20, barely spoke all session. Near end: "I just got my name changed on my ID. First time I have an ID that's actually me." "Like I exist now."
38. C Guy at laptop: "The problem isn't getting people to vote. It's giving people something to vote for."
39. D 11:15 PM. Quick poll, 8 people. 6 registered, 1 not a citizen, 1 unsure. Of 6, 4 voted last election. 2 didn't. Both said deliberate choice.
40. A Person, mid-30s. "Access isn't my problem. I have a car, I have an ID. My problem is I access a system that doesn't want me in it and nothing changes. That's a trust problem."
41. B Resource center exterior. Nondescript. Small rainbow sticker. No signage. Shared lot with a laundromat.
42. E End of meeting, facilitator handed out grocery bags. Canned goods, hygiene products. No sign-up, no paperwork. Five people took one.
43. C Closing time. Barista knew several customers by name. One to another: "See you Saturday?" "Yeah, I'm bringing the signs."
44. D 11:30 PM. Told bartender my project was voting access. "Access to what, though?" Walked away.
45. A Outside, after midnight. Two people discussing a friend's eviction. "We gotta figure out the deposit before Friday." Other one on Venmo.
