# Problem Definition Report

## Overview

Synthesize your team's empathy work (stakeholder mapping, [[A03-Causal-Loop-Map|causal loop analysis]], and observations) into a clear, bounded problem definition. This report serves as the foundation for your [[A05-Mid-Project-Presentation|Mid-Project Gallery Walk]] and [[A06-Final-Presentation|Tell the Story of Your Project]].

## Learning Objectives

- Synthesize diverse research findings into a coherent problem statement
- Articulate a bounded, specific, and actionable problem
- Connect problem definition to evidence from empathy work
- Demonstrate traceability from observation to problem framing

## What to Do

1. **Write a problem statement** that identifies who experiences the problem, what it is (observable, not assumed), how it affects stakeholders (including how it makes them feel), why it matters, and what's in/out of scope.
2. **Summarize your evidence.** Key findings from stakeholder mapping, insights from [[A03-Causal-Loop-Map|causal loop analysis]], and observations from your team's research. Make clear your definition is grounded in evidence.
3. **Analyze stakeholder tensions.** Where do interests conflict? What trade-offs exist? Who would need to change behavior for the problem to be solved?
4. **Identify 2-3 initial solution directions** worth investigating. For each, note which stakeholder insight points toward that direction.

## Submit

Problem Definition Report (PDF) to Canvas before Session 9 (Wednesday, February 18).

## Specifications

**1. Problem Statement Quality**
Met: Identifies specific stakeholders, an observable problem, and clear scope. Problem is bounded and could guide solution development.

**2. Evidence Grounding**
Met: Includes specific findings from team research (quotes, observations, examples). Clear connection between research and problem definition.

**3. Stakeholder Tension Analysis**
Met: Identifies genuine tensions between stakeholder groups. Explains why the problem persists and why solutions may face resistance.

**4. Document Completeness**
Met: All four sections present and complete. Writing is clear and professional, with sufficient depth to demonstrate genuine engagement.
