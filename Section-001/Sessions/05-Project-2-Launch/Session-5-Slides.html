Choosing Your Subject

Scenario = broad problem space. Subject = your team's specific investigation.

Scenario + Subject = Your Investigation

Example: Scenario A (First 90 Days) + "How do new nurses navigate their first rotation at a community hospital?"

  • Access to real stakeholders (you need to talk to people)
  • Bounded scope (one organization, location, or population)
  • Genuine curiosity (not just what seems important)
  • No more than 3 stakeholder groups
"The scenario is the space. The subject is where you go within it."

Choose Your Problem Space

  • 6 scenarios across business and nonprofit contexts
  • Each provides a broad problem space with room to focus
  • You will narrow your scope within the scenario
  • Read the one-pagers, then decide

Scenarios on the next slides.

A: The First 90 Days

Business
"How do new employees learn to navigate an organization?"
  • Choose an organization type
  • Explore the new employee experience
  • What is not taught? Who helps?
  • What falls through the cracks?

B: Small Business Friction

Business
"What makes running a small business harder than it needs to be?"
  • Choose a small business type
  • Explore operational friction
  • What systems don't work together?
  • What workarounds exist?

C: The Feedback Gap

Business
"How do organizations learn what's not working?"
  • Explore how feedback flows (or doesn't)
  • Between customers, frontline staff, and decision-makers
  • Where does information get lost or distorted?

D: Access Barriers

Nonprofit / Social Impact
"Why can't people access services designed for them?"
  • Choose a service type
  • Explore what prevents access
  • What constraints does each stakeholder group face?

E: The Volunteer Experience

Nonprofit / Social Impact
"What makes volunteering sustainable, or not?"
  • Explore why volunteers stay or leave
  • What tensions exist between volunteers, staff, and those served?

F: Mission vs. Operations

Nonprofit / Social Impact
"How do mission-driven organizations navigate competing pressures?"
  • Explore tensions between mission impact and operational sustainability
  • What trade-offs get made? Who decides?

Form Your Teams

  1. Stand up and move to the scenario station that interests you
  2. Faculty will rebalance for team size and diversity
  3. Hard cap: 7 teams

Once your team is set:

  • Exchange contact info
  • Pick a team name (optional but encouraged)
~10 minutes

Start Exploring

With your new team, discuss:

  1. What do you already know about this problem?
  2. What assumptions are you making?
  3. Who are the stakeholders? (List at least 3)
  4. What will you investigate first?
  5. What don't you know that you need to find out?

Document your answers. You will build on this.

~35 minutes

Scope Constraints

  • Maximum 3 stakeholder groups
  • Bounded context (pick a specific organization, location, or population)
  • You don't need to solve everything
  • Depth over breadth
"The tighter your scope, the deeper your insight."
