# Mid-Project Gallery Walk

## Overview

Set up a station for the class gallery walk that shares your problem definition and early solution concepts. This is a checkpoint, not a final pitch: show your thinking, receive written peer feedback, and identify what needs more work before the final session.

## Learning Objectives

- Communicate problem definition clearly through visual materials
- Demonstrate connection between empathy work and problem framing
- Present early solution concepts grounded in research
- Receive and integrate written peer feedback
- Practice giving constructive, specific feedback to other teams

## What to Do

1. **Prepare a station** (table or wall space) with three elements:
   - Problem definition: who, what, why, and what makes it hard to solve
   - Research highlights: key quotes, observations, your [[A03-Causal-Loop-Map|causal loop map]], any empathy work artifacts
   - 1-2 early solution directions with the research insight behind each
2. **Post a specific question** you want visitors to answer about your direction.
3. **During the walk**, leave one team member at your station at all times (rotate halfway). Remaining members visit all other stations.
4. **Leave written feedback** at each station you visit: Where was the connection clearest? What questions do you have? What suggestion would you offer?

Materials should be readable without extensive verbal explanation. Printed or hand-drawn materials work well alongside digital elements.

## Submit

Station set up by 2:20 PM, Session 10 (Monday, February 23). Upload station materials (photos or files) to Canvas after session.

## Specifications

**1. Problem Clarity**
Met: Problem is clearly articulated at the station. Visitors can understand who experiences it, what it is, and why it matters without extensive explanation.

**2. Research Connection**
Met: Station includes specific observations, stakeholder insights, or research findings. Problem definition is visibly grounded in evidence.

**3. Solution Grounding**
Met: Solution directions connect to specific research insights, with explanations of why research points toward these directions.

**4. Station Quality**
Met: Station is well-organized and understandable by visitors with minimal guidance. Materials are readable and visually clear.

**5. Peer Feedback Participation**
Met: Team visits all stations and leaves substantive, specific written feedback at each.
