# Design Thinking Notebook

## Overview

Submit your notebook containing the raw material of your design thinking practice: observations, sketches, maps, reflections, and in-class activities. The notebook values process over presentation and sustained engagement over perfect documentation.

## Learning Objectives

- Maintain a continuous practice of documentation throughout the design process
- Capture observations, ideas, and reflections in real-time
- Create a personal reference for design thinking practices
- Develop the habit of externalizing thinking

## What to Do

Your notebook should include these core entries:

1. **[[A01-TETHER-Observation|TETHER Observation]]**: Full 90-minute observation notes, assumptions in margins, and reflection.
2. **Find the Duct Tape (weekly)**: Each week, document one "duct tape" solution you encounter (a workaround or improvised fix). Record what it is, what problem it solves, and why the real fix doesn't exist.
3. **Individual research notes (Sessions 3-4)**: Observation logs, interview notes, key quotes, insights, and surprises from your [[A03-Stakeholder-Research|Stakeholder Research]].
4. **Stakeholder map drafts** from your team project.
5. **Causal loop drafts** and iterations of your [[A04-Causal-Loop-Map|causal loop map]].
6. **Problem definition iterations** showing how your thinking evolved.

In-class notes are optional but helpful for demonstrating engagement (activity notes, ideation outputs, feedback received, reflections).

Submit as PDF: physical notebooks scanned with pages in order, digital notebooks with clear organization by session/activity.

## Submit

Complete notebook (PDF) to Canvas before Session 6 (Thursday, April 9).

## Specifications

**1. TETHER Documentation**
Met: Substantial TETHER observation notes (3-4+ pages) reflecting 90 minutes of sustained observation, with assumptions noted and reflection present.

**2. Session Coverage**
Met: Entries from each major course phase: Session 1 (ambiguity), Session 2 (observation/team formation), Session 3 (stakeholder mapping), research period, Session 4 (synthesis), Session 5 (presentations/stress test).

**3. Sustained Engagement**
Met: Entries show real-time documentation (context-specific details, natural style variation across sessions, not reconstructible in one sitting).

**4. Thinking Made Visible**
Met: Shows actual thinking through drafts, iterations, questions, and dead ends. Includes visual thinking (stakeholder maps, causal loop attempts, ideation sketches).

**5. Legibility and Organization**
Met: Notebook is readable, pages in chronological order, and clear enough to identify which entries correspond to which activities.
