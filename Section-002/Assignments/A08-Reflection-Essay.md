# Reflection Essay

## Overview

Write a 500-800 word reflection that closes the course by examining your design thinking learning and how you'll apply it professionally. Consider which design abilities resonated, where you tend to jump to solutions, and how distributed research across diverse environments enriched your team's work.

## Learning Objectives

- Reflect on personal growth across the 7 design abilities
- Connect design thinking practices to professional contexts
- Evaluate how distributed research and diverse team contexts strengthened problem understanding
- Articulate transferable skills from the course

## What to Do

1. **Reflect on design abilities.** Which of the 7 design abilities felt most natural? Most challenging? What specific moment or activity shifted your thinking?
2. **Connect to professional application.** Where in your professional context do you jump to solutions too quickly? How might you apply "Navigate Ambiguity" or "Learn From Others" at work? How could you use observation or stakeholder mapping in your role?
3. **Evaluate distributed research.** What did you learn from teammates' diverse contexts during the research period? How did different industries, cities, or environments enrich your understanding? What would have been different if everyone researched in the same location?

## Submit

PDF or Word document to Canvas before Session 6 (Thursday, April 9). 500-800 words addressing all three parts.

## Specifications

**1. Design Ability Reflection**
Met: Identifies specific abilities that felt natural and challenging with concrete examples from the course. References specific moments or activities.

**2. Professional Application**
Met: Provides concrete examples of where design thinking could be applied in their professional context. Identifies specific situations where they jump to solutions.

**3. Distributed Research Reflection**
Met: Articulates what was learned from teammates' different contexts and how diverse environments enriched the team's understanding.

**4. Depth and Specificity**
Met: Goes beyond surface-level observations with specific details, examples, and moments from the course. Demonstrates synthesis across the full course arc.

**5. Length and Completeness**
Met: 500-800 words. All three parts addressed. Writing is clear and organized.
