Distributed Research Guidelines

Why Distributed Research?

In a hybrid course, team members access different workplaces, industries, cities, and communities. This geographic and professional diversity is a research asset.

Planning Your Team's Research

  1. Map your combined reach: What contexts can each member access?
  2. Identify stakeholders: Which stakeholders can each member observe or interview?
  3. Divide responsibilities: Maximize coverage while avoiding duplication
  4. Set coordination rhythm: How will you check in between Sessions 3 and 4?

Coordination Tips

  • Create a shared document early (Google Doc, Notion, etc.)
  • Set up a team channel (Slack, GroupMe, text thread)
  • Schedule at least one video check-in during the research period
  • Share findings as you goâ€”don't wait until the deadline

Quality Over Quantity

  • Each member: 1-2 substantial observations or interviews
  • Focus on depth, not breadth
  • Document in your notebook; compile for team
  • Note what's unique to your context vs. what might be universal

Before Session 4

  • Compile all findings into team document
  • Look for patterns across contexts
  • Note where different contexts revealed different facets of the problem
  • Identify remaining questions and assumptions
