## Topics

- Generating and stress testing solution hypotheses
- Assumption auditing and kill criteria
- Designing credible experiments you can run in 5 days
- Leveraging your distributed contexts for real-world testing

## What We'll Do

**Morning (8:00-9:30)**
- Problem Definition feedback: I'll return comments on each team's doc and revised causal loop.
- **Rough Pitches** (gallery walk): Present your problem and initial solution hypotheses. Other teams stress test your thinking.
- **Assumption Audit**: Identify your 5 most critical assumptions. Rank them. Define kill criteria.

**Late Morning (9:45-12:30)**
- **Adversarial Stress Test**: Teams pair up and try to break each other's assumptions. Find weaknesses, not strengths.
- **Iterate**: Revise your framing or hypotheses based on what broke.
- **Design the Experiment**: Build a credible experiment plan your team can execute in 5 days. What will you test, with whom, how will you measure, and what would disprove your hypothesis? Assign specific tasks to each team member based on who can reach which stakeholders.
- **Share Experiment Plans**: Peer feasibility check. "Is this actually testable in 5 days?"
